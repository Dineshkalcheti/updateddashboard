02 · Behavioral Analysis

How AI dependency is reshaping decision behavior

When AI is removed, youth struggle. Decision delays multiply. Confidence collapses. Verification is almost non-existent.

Visualization 3 · Decision delay grows with reliance
Average seconds to decide without AI — by reliance level
Level 1: 58.9s → Level 5: 277.7s — a 4.7× increase. AI has replaced youth thinking speed entirely.
Visualization 4 · Confidence without AI collapses
Average decision confidence (1–5) by reliance level
Level 1: 4.32/5 → Level 5: 1.33/5. Youth who are highly dependent don't trust their own judgment at all.
Visualization 5 · Verification frequency distribution
How often youth verify AI outputs (0–10 scale)
531 youth (35.4%) verify 0 times. 1,001 verify ≤ 1 time — the single most critical behavioral risk in the dataset.
Visualization 6 · Misinformation risk
Times per week youth acted on incorrect AI output without verifying
560 youth (37.3%) follow at least 1 AI error per week without checking.

04 · Physical Wellness

AI is leaving a physical mark on youth health

154 minutes of daily screen time is producing measurable physical consequences.

Visualization 9 · Screen time vs eye strain (scatter)
Each dot = 1 youth · dose-response relationship confirmed
Every High eye strain case occurs above 200 min/day — direct evidence base for the 90-min daily AI screen limit.
Visualization 10 · Eye strain distribution
Count at each level from the full 1,500-record dataset
274 youth (18.3%) have medium or high eye strain at only 154 min/day average — will rise as AI programs expand.
Visualization 11 · Screen time distribution
How many youth exceed safe limits?
464 youth (30.9%) exceed 180 min/day. Physical discomfort is measurably higher above this threshold.
Visualization 12 · Physical stiffness by AI tool
% reporting stiffness — screen duration is the driver, not tool type
458 youth (30.5%) report physical stiffness. Rates consistent across all tool types — screen time is the cause.
05 · AI Tool Patterns

Not all AI tools create the same dependency

Passive tools that make decisions for users produce deeper dependency than active tools that assist user thinking.

Visualization 13 · Average reliance by tool type
Mean AI_Reliance_Level (1–5) per primary tool
Recommendation Systems (1.55) and Navigation AI (1.51) produce the highest dependency — both are passive decision-making tools.
Visualization 14 · Trust paradox
Higher trust does NOT increase verification — it peaks then drops
Trust Level 5 verifies 1.67/day — LESS than Trust Level 4 (2.18/day). The most trusting youth skip verification entirely.
